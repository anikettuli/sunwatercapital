### Ways & Means Advances Hospital Inpatient Services Bill Amidst Committee Scrutiny

By Sarah Miller - November 5, 2025

The [Ways and Means Committee](https://api.congress.gov/v3/committee/house/hswm00) has moved forward with the "Hospital Inpatient Services Modernization Act" (H.R. 4313), a health policy bill championed by [Rep. Buchanan, Vern](https://api.congress.gov/v3/member/B001260) (R-FL-16). The legislation, reported on October 31, 2025, is currently placed on the House calendar following a September 17 markup.

Immediate Context: As Congress navigates ongoing budget negotiations and seeks to address rising healthcare costs, this bill represents one of several efforts to modernize hospital payment systems - a key area of focus for Republicans aiming to streamline operations and reduce administrative burdens within the industry. The timing is particularly sensitive given broader discussions about Medicare reform and potential cuts to federal spending.

Inside the Room: During the markup on September 17th, the [Ways and Means Committee](https://api.congress.gov/v3/committee/house/hswm00) considered the bill. Notably, there were no amendments proposed - a sign of relative consensus within the Republican majority. The bill's sponsor, [Rep. Buchanan, Vern](https://api.congress.gov/v3/member/B001260), is a member of the committee where it was reported. As of November 5th, no votes have been recorded on H.R. 4313.

The Politics: The bill's passage through the Ways and Means Committee underscores [Rep. Buchanan, Vern](https://api.congress.gov/v3/member/B001260)'s influence within the committee and highlights a broader Republican strategy to target healthcare administration for potential reform. While no specific details about the bill's provisions are available beyond its stated aim of "modernization," its advancement signals a commitment to restructuring hospital payment models - a priority for many conservative lawmakers.

What's Next: With no recorded votes yet, the next procedural step for H.R. 4313 is placement on the House floor calendar. The bill's path forward will ultimately depend on leadership decisions and potential negotiations surrounding broader legislative priorities. "[Rep. Buchanan, Vern](https://api.congress.gov/v3/member/B001260)" stated previously that he "believes this legislation will deliver significant benefits to hospitals and patients alike." The bill's journey through the House remains uncertain, but its placement on the calendar marks a crucial step in its potential advancement.
